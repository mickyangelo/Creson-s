Explore Benin City

We are so excited to welcome you to the ancient and beautiful city of Benin. While you are here for our celebration, we hope you take a moment to explore the rich history and culture this land has to offer. Here are our top 4 favorite spots.

Benin City National Museum

Situated in the heart of the city at King‚Äôs Square, this is the custodian of the Edo people‚Äôs history. It houses a priceless collection of the famous Benin Bronzes, terracotta figures, and cast iron artifacts that date back centuries. It is the perfect starting point to understand the depth of the culture we are celebrating today.

● King's Square, Ring Road, Benin City

The Royal Oba's Palace

A UNESCO Listed Heritage Site, the Oba‚Äôs Palace is the spiritual and cultural heartbeat of the Benin Kingdom. Rebuilt in 1914, it stands on grounds that have been the seat of power for over 900 years. The traditional architecture and the sheer aura of royalty make this a must-visit for anyone who appreciates living history.

● Adesogbe Road, Benin City

Igun Street (Bronze Casters)

Walk down the red earth of Igun Street and step back in time. This is the exclusive home of the guild of bronze casters, a craft passed down from father to son for generations. Watch master artisans shape molten metal into world-renowned sculptures just as they did in the 14th century. It is the perfect place to pick up a timeless souvenir.

● Igun Street, off Sakponba Road

Ogba Zoo & Nature Park

For a breath of fresh air, escape to the Ogba Zoo. It is one of the few natural reserves in an urban setting, featuring a mix of rich wildlife including lions, chimpanzees, and giant tortoises and serene bamboo landscapes. It is a lovely spot for a quiet afternoon walk or a picnic before the wedding festivities begin.

● Airport Road, Ogba, Benin City

Kada Cinemas is a prominent cinema and family entertainment center located in Benin City.
